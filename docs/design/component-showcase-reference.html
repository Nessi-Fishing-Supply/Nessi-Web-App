Navigation System
\n
Three persistent elements form the navigation shell. None of them ever hide, animate away, or get replaced by a custom header. The pattern is identical on every screen — only the active state changes.
\n\n
▲ Decision: Why Account lives top-right, not in the bottom bar
\n
\nAccount has the most functionality behind it: listings, orders, payouts, shop tools, upgrade flows, settings. It needs to be always reachable but not competing for navigation priority with the app's primary journeys. Top-right avatar solves this — it's one thumb-stretch, always in frame, and the avatar/dot acts as a persistent signed-in signal. Putting Account in the bottom bar wastes a slot on a destination people visit occasionally, not continuously. Bottom bar slots are reserved for journeys users enter repeatedly throughout a session.

\nThis mirrors Etsy's pattern: Account top-right, 5 bottom slots for primary navigation. The difference: Nessi's Sell FAB occupies the center slot — it needs to be the most prominent element on screen for sellers.\n
